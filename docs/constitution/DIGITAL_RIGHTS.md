# Digital Rights Charter

> **Fundamental digital rights for every citizen of Digital Nepal**

## Preamble

In the digital age, the rights of citizens must be protected with the same vigor as physical rights. The AsimNexus Digital Rights Charter establishes the fundamental digital rights that are inviolable, inalienable, and enforceable for every citizen of Digital Nepal.

## Article I: Data Sovereignty

### Section 1: Ownership

Every citizen has complete and exclusive ownership of their personal data. This includes:

1. **Identity Data** — Name, address, biometrics, and government IDs
2. **Behavioral Data** — Usage patterns, preferences, and activity logs
3. **Financial Data** — Transaction history, balances, and credit information
4. **Health Data** — Medical records, diagnoses, and treatment history
5. **Communication Data** — Messages, calls, and social interactions

### Section 2: Control

Citizens have the right to:

1. **Access** — View all data stored about them at any time
2. **Portability** — Export their data in standard formats
3. **Rectification** — Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
4. **Erasure** — Delete their data (right to be forgotten)
5. **Restriction** — Limit how their data is processed
6. **Objection** — Object to specific data processing activities

### Section 3: Consent

1. **Explicit Consent** — All data processing requires explicit, informed consent
2. **Granular Consent** — Citizens can consent to specific processing activities independently
3. **Revocable Consent** — Consent can be withdrawn at any time
4. **No Lock-In** — Withholding consent cannot result in service denial for essential services

## Article II: Privacy Rights

### Section 1: Private Mode

Every citizen has the right to operate in Private Mode, which guarantees:

1. **End-to-End Encryption** — All communications are encrypted
2. **No Tracking** — No behavioral tracking or profiling
3. **Anonymous Transactions** — Transactions are pseudonymous
4. **Local Processing** — Data processing occurs on-device when possible

### Section 2: Surveillance Protection

1. **No Warrantless Surveillance** — Government surveillance requires judicial warrant
2. **Transparency** — All surveillance activities are logged and auditable
3. **Notification** — Citizens are notified of surveillance after it concludes
4. **Remediation** — Citizens have recourse for unlawful surveillance

## Article III: Freedom of Digital Expression

### Section 1: Communication

1. **Free Communication** — Citizens may communicate freely through any channel
2. **No Censorship** — No prior restraint on digital expression
3. **Anonymous Speech** — Citizens may speak anonymously
4. **Platform Neutrality** — No discrimination based on content or viewpoint

### Section 2: Assembly

1. **Digital Assembly** — Citizens may form digital groups and communities
2. **No Surveillance** — Digital assemblies are not subject to special surveillance
3. **Autonomous Moderation** — Communities set their own moderation rules

## Article IV: Right to Digital Identity

### Section 1: Self-Sovereign Identity

1. **Self-Managed** — Citizens control their own identity credentials
2. **Selective Disclosure** — Citizens can reveal only necessary attributes
3. **Portable Identity** — Identity works across all system services
4. **Recovery** — Citizens can recover their identity through biometric or social recovery

### Section 2: Pseudonymity

1. **Multiple Identities** — Citizens may maintain multiple pseudonymous identities
2. **No Forced Linking** — Pseudonyms cannot be forcibly linked to real identities
3. **Reputation Portability** — Reputation can be transferred between pseudonyms

## Article V: Right to Human Oversight

### Section 1: AI Decision-Making

1. **Human Review** — Citizens can request human review of AI decisions
2. **Explanation** — Citizens are entitled to explanations of AI decisions
3. **Appeal** — Citizens can appeal AI decisions to human authorities
4. **Opt-Out** — Citizens can opt out of AI-only decision-making for critical matters

### Section 2: Agent Contracts

1. **Informed Consent** — Citizens must explicitly consent to agent representation
2. **Duration Limits** — Agent contracts are limited to 5/15/30 day durations
3. **Revocation** — Citizens can revoke agent authority at any time
4. **Audit Trail** — All agent actions are logged and reviewable

## Article VI: Right to Offline Access

### Section 1: Offline-First Design

1. **Offline Functionality** — Core services work without internet connectivity
2. **Mesh Networking** — Citizens can connect via peer-to-peer mesh networks
3. **Local Storage** — Data is stored locally by default
4. **Sync on Connect** — Automatic synchronization when connectivity is available

### Section 2: No Connectivity Discrimination

1. **Equal Access** — Offline users receive the same service quality
2. **No Penalty** — No penalties for intermittent connectivity
3. **Async Operations** — All operations support asynchronous execution

## Article VII: Enforcement

### Section 1: Digital Rights Commission

An independent Digital Rights Commission shall:

1. Investigate violations of digital rights
2. Issue binding remediation orders
3. Impose penalties for violations
4. Report annually on the state of digital rights

### Section 2: Remedies

Citizens whose digital rights are violated are entitled to:

1. **Cessation** — Immediate stop to the violation
2. **Restitution** — Restoration of the prior state
3. **Compensation** — Monetary compensation for damages
4. **Publication** — Public acknowledgment of the violation
